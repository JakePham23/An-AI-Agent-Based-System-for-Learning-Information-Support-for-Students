## LEARNING OBJECTIVES (CLOs)
- G1.Understand the basic knowledge of philosophy in general and the conditions for the emergence of Marxist-Leninist philosophy. At the same time, assist students in analyzing and evaluating the essence of the revolution in philosophy carried out by Karl Marx and Friedrich Engels, as well as the stages of the formation and development of Marxist-Leninist philosophy. Additionally, emphasize the role of Marxist philosophy in social life and in the modern era.
- G2.Understand the dialectical materialist view of matter, the forms and modes of existence of matter, the origin and nature of consciousness, and the dialectical relationship between matter and consciousness. Assist students in understanding the fundamental contents of dialectical materialism, as well as its methodological significance in cognition and practice. Help students grasp the basic knowledge of the theory of cognition in dialectical materialism and its methodological significance."
- G3.Understand the knowledge of the socio-economic formation theory and apply its methodological significance to the practical context of Vietnam. Assist students in understanding the basic viewpoints of Marxist-Leninist philosophy regarding classes, the state, social revolution, nations, and the relationships among classes, nations, and humanity, as well as the methodological significance in comprehending the fundamental issues of the Vietnamese revolution. Help students grasp the fundamental viewpoints of Marxist-Leninist philosophy regarding human beings, social consciousness, and its application to the Vietnamese revolution."
- G8.Analyze the scientific and revolutionary foundations of dialectical materialism; and struggle against the erroneous views of idealism and metaphysical materialism.
- G9.Adopt a Marxist stance, reinforce their belief in the scientific and revolutionary nature of historical materialism, trust in the Party's policies and viewpoints, and struggle against hostile and erroneous opinions to defend the ideological foundation of the Party.
