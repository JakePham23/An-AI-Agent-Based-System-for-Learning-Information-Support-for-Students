- Concepts and Foundations of Environmental Science
- Earth in Space (Solar System, Universe, Life Cycle of Stars)
- Physical and Chemical Properties, Composition, Origin, and Age of Earth
- Composition and Structure of the Atmosphere; Water in the Atmosphere
- Wind and Weather
- Energy in the Atmosphere; Climate Change and Air Pollution
- Freshwater on Earth
- Seas and Oceans
- Surface Materials of the Earth: Minerals, Soil, and Rocks – Weathering, Erosion, and Accumulation (Gravitational Activities)
- Earthquakes, Volcanoes, and Plate Tectonics
- History of the Earth
- Minerals and Energy on Earth

## LEARNING OBJECTIVES (CLOs)
- G2.Describe the position of Earth in space and its relationship with other objects in the Solar System. Present the characteristics regarding the shape, physical and chemical properties, structure, origin, and age of the Earth
- G3.Present the composition and properties of the components of the Earth (the Earth's spheres) and explain the natural phenomena occurring within these spheres, as well as the laws of interaction between the Earth's spheres
- G4.Understand the history of the Earth through records of fossils and stratigraphy
- G5.Apply this knowledge to explain certain issues in the main field of study
- G6.Have skills in group discussion, presentation, and critical thinking
- G7.Attitude: enthusiastic, honest in learning; serious and truthful in examinations
