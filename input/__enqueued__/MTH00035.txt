## COURSE DESCRIPTION
(1) Learn about linear equations: Students will study the theory and techniques related to solving systems of linear equations. They will explore concepts such as matrix operations, row reduction, and Gaussian elimination to solve linear equations and analyze their solutions.
(2) Gain proficiency in matrix methods: Students will develop a strong understanding of matrices and their properties. They will learn how to perform operations on matrices, such as matrix addition, multiplication, and inversion. Additionally, they will explore applications of matrices in various fields.
(3) Explore analytical geometry: Students will delve into the study of vectors and their geometric interpretations. They will learn about vector operations, vector spaces, and vector equations. This knowledge will provide a foundation for understanding geometric concepts and their relationship to linear algebra.
(4) Understand linear transformations: Students will examine the concept of linear transformations and their properties. They will explore the relationship between linear transformations and matrices, as well as the applications of linear transformations.
By the end of the course, students will have a solid understanding of linear algebra concepts and their applications in mathematics, engineering, and the sciences. They will be equipped with the necessary knowledge to solve linear equations, perform matrix operations, analyze vector spaces, and comprehend the role of linear transformations. This knowledge will serve as a valuable foundation for their future studies.

## LEARNING OBJECTIVES (CLOs)
- G1.Recognize and explain the English terms related to the subject.
- G2.Practice effectively solving and reasoning through systems of linear equations.
- G3.Practice well with matrix calculations and know how to apply invertible matrices to solve matrix equations.
- G4.Practice calculating the determinant of a matrix to use it for solving systems of linear equations, finding the rank of a matrix, examining invertibility, and finding the inverse matrix.
- G8.Recognize ethical principles and responsibilities in learning.
- G9.Creative thinking and strong critical thinking skills. Maintain a serious attitude towards learning and self-study.
- G5.Apply knowledge of systems of linear equations, matrix operations, invertible matrices, and matrix determinants to describe the structure of vector spaces and related issues (subspaces, generating sets, linearly independent or dependent sets, finding bases for subspaces, vector coordinates relative to a basis, change of basis matrices, and coordinate transformation formulas).
- G6.Proficiently apply vector spaces to study linear mappings between vector spaces and related issues (basis of the product space, basis of the image space, matrix representation of linear mappings, formulas for changing matrix representations between bases, and finding linear mappings based on the image of a basis).
- G7.Effectively use knowledge of systems of linear equations, matrix determinants, and vector spaces to introduce eigenvalues, eigenspaces, eigenvectors, and the characteristic polynomial of square matrices. From there, define the concept of diagonalizable matrices, the necessary and sufficient conditions for diagonalizability, diagonalization algorithms, and applications of diagonalizable matrices.
