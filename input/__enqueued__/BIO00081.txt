- **Semester**: (s) in which the module is taught: 2

## COURSE DESCRIPTION
• COURSE TOPICS:
- Exercise 1: Theory of errors, graphs, data analysis, instructions for using instruments and equipment.
- Exercise 2: Measuring the specific gravity and density of liquids and solids.
- Exercise 3: Investigating the viscosity of liquids.
- Exercise 4: Measuring gravitational acceleration using a reversible pendulum.
- Exercise 5: Investigating the polarization of light – Verifying Malus's law.
- Exercise 6: Investigating the phenomenon of light dispersion: Measuring the refractive index of a glass prism.
- Exercise 7: Measuring thermal energy – The melting heat of ice.
- Exercise 8: Measuring the mechanical equivalent of a calorie.
- Exercise 9: Investigating the volt-ampere characteristic curve of diodes and transistors.
- Exercise 10: Investigating DC and AC circuits, RLC circuits.
- Exercise 11: Measuring the resistance of various types of resistors using the Wheatstone bridge method.
- Exercise 12: Measuring small objects using a microscope.
- Introduction to the subject
- Laboratory regulations
- Microscope and cell structure
- Osmosis
- Organic components of the cell
- Enzyme activity
- Cellular respiration
- Mitosis
- Microbial diversity
- Plant diversity
- Animal diversity

## LEARNING OBJECTIVES (CLOs)
- G1.Prepare microscopic slides and use optical microscopes.
- G2.Conduct experiments to demonstrate some basic life processes: osmosis, enzyme activity, respiration, and mitosis.
- G3.Perform some qualitative reactions and identify the basic organic components of life.
- G4.Observe and recognize the main groups of animals, plants, and microorganisms.
- G5.Use the basic equipment and instruments of a general biology laboratory.
- G6.Comply with laboratory rules and follow biosafety regulations.
